Aircraft noise level unit and characteristic (WECPNL)
  • Weighted Equivalent Continuous Perceived Noise Level: aircraft noise level measurement unit suggested by the International Civil Aviation Organization (ICAO)
  • Comprehensive measurement of the sound level upon takeoff and landing, frequency of flights, flight time, and maximum sound level and different from decibel (dB), which only measures how loud a sound is. For example, even with same volume, the noise emission between 10 p.m. and 7 a.m. is considered to have a negative effect that is 10 times as great as a flight that operates during the day (7 a.m.–7 p.m.). Calculation consists of adding points depending on the time of operation and the maximum average dB of the flight. Flights in the evening (7 p.m.-10 p.m.) is three times the penal points and late night (10 p.m.-7 a.m.) receives 10 times the penal points.
  • According to the current aviation laws, regions exposed to more than 80 WECPNL is considered Expected Noise Damage Area and more than 90 WECPNL is considered Noise Damage Area. In such a case, the responsible government agency must develop and implement migration policy or countermeasures.
